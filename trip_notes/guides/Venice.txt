Visiting Venice feels like stepping into a living museum, where romantic canals replace streets and stunning Renaissance architecture lines every waterway. The floating city is a labyrinth of charming bridges, narrow cobblestone alleys, and picturesque piazzas that invite endless exploration. The ideal time to visit is during the shoulder seasons of spring from April to May or early autumn from September to October, when the weather is pleasant and the massive summer crowds have somewhat thinned out. You must try cicchetti, which are traditional Venetian tapas served in local wine bars called bacari. Another regional specialty is sarde in saor, delicious sweet and sour sardines, perfectly paired with a classic Aperol Spritz. Getting lost in the winding streets is an activity in itself, but you should also take a Vaporetto water bus ride down the Grand Canal to take in the majestic palazzos. Exploring the vibrant Rialto Market early in the morning offers a fantastic glimpse into authentic local life. For student travelers looking to save money, avoid dining in major tourist squares where prices are heavily inflated; instead, seek out small bacari in the Dorsoduro district. Also, skip the expensive private gondolas and opt for a traghetto, a cheap shared gondola used by locals to quickly cross the Grand Canal.
